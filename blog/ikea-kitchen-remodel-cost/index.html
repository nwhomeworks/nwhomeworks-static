How Much Does an IKEA Kitchen Remodel Cost? [2026 Pricing Guide]

April 16, 2026 • By Tim Hutchinson

IKEA kitchens have a reputation for being the budget option — and they can be. But “budget” doesn’t mean cheap, and the total cost of an IKEA kitchen remodel surprises a lot of people once you add up installation labor, countertops, electrical, plumbing, and all the finishing work that IKEA doesn’t include.

As the owner of NW Homeworks, I’ve installed literally hundreds of IKEA kitchens across Seattle, Tacoma, Bellevue, and the greater Puget Sound area over the past 11 years. Some came in under $12,000. Others pushed past $70,000 and looked like $150,000 custom kitchens when they were done. The difference in price comes down to scope, not necessarily quality.

Here’s a real world look at what an IKEA kitchen remodel costs in 2026, broken down by tier, by category, and with a direct comparison to conventional cabinetry.

IKEA Kitchen Remodel Cost by Tier

Here’s the quick reference:

TierCost RangeWhat You’re Getting
Basic Remodel$10,000–$15,000Old cabinet removal, IKEA SEKTION cabinet install, butcher block or laminate countertops, basic backsplash. Keep existing appliances, and no electrical or plumbing changes.
Mid-Range Remodel$25,000–$40,000Everything above plus quartz countertops, tile backsplash, new appliances, some electrical and plumbing work.
High-End IKEA Remodel$40,000–$70,000Layout changes, wall removal, custom doors on IKEA boxes, new flooring, new trim, higher-end appliances, electrical and plumbing upgrades.

These ranges are based on our actual Seattle-area projects and assume a kitchen around 100–140 square feet. Older homes — craftsmans, post-war bungalows, anything pre-1970 — almost always add cost because walls are out of plumb, floors slope, and the plumbing and electrical usually need bringing up to code.

What Makes IKEA Kitchens Cheaper (and Where They’re Not)

The IKEA SEKTION system is genuinely well-engineered for the price. The cabinet boxes are just particle board with a melamine finish, but they’re solid and hold up really well over time. Where they shine is the hardware – smooth, soft-close undermount drawer slides and soft-close hinges that rival the feel of even high-end custom cabinetry. For the money, the quality is genuinely hard to beat.

Where people miscalculate is installation labor. IKEA cabinets are flat-pack, and assembling and installing them takes more labor hours per cabinet than hanging a pre-assembled semi-stock box. That means the installation cost per square foot is actually higher than mid-range conventional cabinets, even though the cabinet cost itself is significantly lower.

The net result: you still save money overall with IKEA, but not as dramatically as you might expect just looking at cabinet prices.

The Custom Door Hack: High-End Look, IKEA Price

One of the most popular builds we do is pairing IKEA SEKTION boxes with custom-built cabinet doors and drawer fronts. You get the price and engineering of IKEA’s cabinet system with the look of fully custom cabinetry.

Common builds we do:

  • Walnut slab
  • White oak shaker
  • Painted MDF in custom colors
  • Mid-century teak
  • Rift-sawn oak flat-front

The custom doors typically add $4,000–$12,000 to the cabinet line item depending on wood species and door count, but the total is still well under what you’d pay for a fully custom cabinet system. A $15,000 IKEA + custom door package looks like a $35,000 custom cabinet job.

What IKEA’s Installation Program Doesn’t Cover

IKEA’s installation service through their third-party program only covers cabinet assembly and mounting. They don’t handle demo, plumbing, electrical, countertops, backsplash, flooring, drywall repair, appliance hookups, or any of the modifications needed for older homes where nothing is square. If you go that route, you’re hiring and scheduling five or six separate trades yourself. For the full breakdown, see our post on whether IKEA does kitchen installation.

A licensed general contractor handles every trade under one roof: one point of contact, one schedule, one crew that knows exactly how IKEA kitchens go together.

IKEA Kitchen Modifications and Hacks That Are Worth the Money

Part of the value of working with an experienced IKEA installer is knowing what modifications are possible and worth the investment. Here are the ones we build most often:

  • Toe-kick drawers — Turn dead space under the cabinets into full-size storage drawers. Adds ~$300–$400 per drawer.
  • Appliance garages — Hidden behind integrated doors using 3rd-party pocket door hardware. Adds ~$250–$300.
  • Stacked uppers to ceiling — IKEA standard uppers leave a gap at the ceiling. We stack them and add custom crown molding for a built-in look. Adds ~$250–$350.
  • Pull-out spice racks — Great for tight spaces. Modify an IKEA frame to 5” width and add a 3rd-party spice rack. Adds ~$350–$400.
  • Integrated appliance panels — Dishwasher, fridge, and range panels that match the cabinetry for a seamless look. Adds ~$150–$250 for installation cost only.

Is an IKEA Kitchen Right for Your Home?

Short answer: yes, for most people, in most situations.

IKEA’s SEKTION system is a genuinely good product at a price point that’s hard to match. The cabinet boxes are well-engineered, the hardware performs well above its price grade, and the modular system is flexible and easy to work with.

The cabinets aren’t the weak point; the installation is. A poorly installed IKEA kitchen looks cheap and won’t hold up over time. A well-installed one not only lasts 15–20 years, but the finishing details — precision scribing, seamless filler panels, crown molding tight to the ceiling — make it look indistinguishable from cabinetry that costs three times as much. Plus, the money saved on cabinets leaves more room in the budget for extras like quartz countertops, a tile backsplash, new appliances, and under-cabinet lighting.

How Long Does an IKEA Kitchen Remodel Take?

  • Basic remodel (cabinet swap, same layout): 1–2 weeks
  • Mid-range (electrical, plumbing, tile, new appliances): 4–6 weeks
  • High-end (layout changes, custom doors, full gut): 6–8 weeks

One advantage of IKEA: no lead time on cabinets. They’re in stock at the store. A custom cabinet order takes 8–16 weeks before anyone can even start the install. With IKEA, we can often begin within a week or two of signing.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does an IKEA kitchen remodel cost?

A full IKEA kitchen remodel typically costs $10,000–$70,000 depending on scope. A basic cabinet swap runs $10,000–$15,000. A mid-range remodel with quartz countertops, tile, electrical, and new appliances runs $25,000–$40,000. High-end IKEA remodels with layout changes and custom doors can reach $40,000–$70,000.

Are IKEA kitchen cabinets good quality?

Yes. IKEA’s SEKTION system is engineered to last 25 years, and they have a warranty to back that. The hardware is honestly top-notch with undermount, soft-close drawer slides and integrated soft-close hinges that rival any of the top brands at any price. In actual use, with proper installation, they hold up very well for 15–20 years with minimum wear and tear. The key is professional installation, especially in older homes where walls aren’t plumb and floors aren’t level.

Is an IKEA kitchen cheaper than custom?

Significantly. For a 120 SF kitchen, IKEA cabinets installed cost roughly $7,200 versus $19,200 for semi-custom and $32,500+ for fully custom. Even with the custom door hack (IKEA boxes + custom-built doors), you’ll typically save 50–60% compared to a fully custom cabinet system.

How much does IKEA kitchen installation cost?

IKEA’s own installation program (cabinet assembly and mounting only) runs roughly $2,000–$4,000. But that doesn’t include demo, plumbing, electrical, countertops, tile, or any finishing work. A full-service installation through a general contractor — everything from demo to final walkthrough — runs $10,000–$70,000 depending on scope.

Can you put custom doors on IKEA cabinets?

Yes — this is one of the most popular builds we do. Custom doors and drawer fronts that are built to fit IKEA SEKTION boxes in any wood species, finish, or profile. It typically adds $4,000–$12,000 to the cabinet line item but delivers a look that rivals $35,000+ custom cabinetry.

How long does an IKEA kitchen remodel take?

A basic cabinet swap takes 1–2 weeks. A mid-range remodel with electrical, plumbing, and tile takes 4–6 weeks. A high-end remodel with layout changes and custom doors takes 6–8 weeks. One big advantage: IKEA cabinets are in stock, so there’s no 8–16 week lead time like custom cabinets.
