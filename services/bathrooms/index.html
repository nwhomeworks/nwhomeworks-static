Bathroom Remodeling Seattle

Your personal retreat, built to last

The Vision

A bathroom remodel is one of the most personal projects in your home. It's the space where you start and end every day — and it should feel like it was designed just for you. Whether you're envisioning a spa-like master bath with a walk-in rain shower, updating a kids' bathroom that's seen better days, or adding a guest bath that impresses, we bring the craftsmanship and care to make it exceptional.

Tile That Sets the Tone

Tile defines a bathroom. It's the first thing you see and the surface you interact with most — and it demands precision. Our in-house tile team brings years of hands-on experience to every shower surround, floor, and accent wall. Whether it's large-format porcelain, intricate mosaic patterns, or hand-glazed artisan tile, they install it with the kind of care that turns a bathroom into a showpiece.

Built for Comfort

A great bathroom isn't just beautiful — it's comfortable. Our electricians install heated flooring that takes the edge off cold mornings, towel warmers that add a touch of luxury, and lighting systems that set the right mood from bright and energizing to soft and relaxing. Our plumbers engineer everything from multi-head shower systems to freestanding tub installations — because what's behind the walls matters just as much as what you see. Every connection is solid, every line is pressure-tested, and every fixture performs as beautifully as it looks.

Custom Details

It's the small, thoughtful touches that elevate a bathroom from nice to truly yours. A perfectly sized recessed shelf in the shower, built in our custom workshop to fit your space exactly. A floating vanity with soft-close drawers. Frameless glass that makes a small bathroom feel twice its size. We sweat these details because they're the difference between a bathroom that simply functions and one that becomes your personal retreat — a space so comfortable and beautiful that a long day melts away the moment you step inside.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does a bathroom remodel take?

A cosmetic update — new tile, vanity, fixtures — usually takes about 3–4 weeks. A full gut where we strip it to the studs and rebuild with new plumbing, heated floors, and custom tile work runs more like 4–6 weeks. Structural changes and major layout revisions can add additional weeks to the project timeline.

Do you do small cosmetic updates or just full bathroom remodels?

We do both, and honestly we enjoy the smaller jobs. A lot of contractors won’t return your call for a vanity swap or a tub-to-shower conversion — we will. Sometimes a targeted update is exactly what a bathroom needs, and there’s real satisfaction in making a big difference with a focused scope. That said, about 70% of our bathroom work ends up being full renovations — strip to the studs, new plumbing, waterproofing, custom tile, the whole thing. We’re happy either way. The right scope is whatever actually makes sense for your space and your budget.

Do I need to move out during a bathroom remodel?

Not usually. If it's a guest bath or kids' bath, life goes on — just use the other bathroom. If it's your only bathroom, that's a different conversation. We can sometimes phase the work to keep the toilet functional for most of the project, but there may be a day or two where you need to make other arrangements. A master bath remodel when you have a second bathroom? No disruption at all — we close the door and you won't even know we're there until the walkthrough.

What’s the typical cost of a bathroom remodel in Seattle?

Depends on scope. A cosmetic update — new tile, vanity, fixtures, paint — the cost of labor and fixtures could run around $12,000–$25,000 in the Seattle market. A mid-range full remodel with new plumbing, tile throughout, and quality fixtures lands between $25,000 and $50,000. A high-end master bath with custom tile, heated floors, frameless glass, and luxury fixtures can push $50,000–$80,000+. Seattle labor rates run 20–30% above national averages, which is the biggest reason local pricing is higher than what you’ll see in national guides.
