IKEA Kitchen Installation Seattle

Smart design, expertly installed

The Smart Choice

IKEA cabinets are one of the best-kept secrets in kitchen remodeling — high quality European-style construction at a fraction of the cost of custom cabinetry. But getting them from flat-pack to flawless takes experience. With hundreds of IKEA kitchens installed across the Puget Sound, we know every quirk, every shortcut to avoid, and every trick that makes the difference between a DIY project and a professional result.

More Than Just Kitchens

IKEA's cabinet system is incredibly versatile — and we've installed them everywhere. Bathroom vanities, laundry rooms, mudroom storage, walk-in closets, home offices, and media centers. If there's a space in your home that needs smart, affordable storage, chances are we've built something like it before. We handle everything from the initial design and layout to the final adjustment, so every door closes perfectly and every drawer glides like it should.

The IKEA Hacks

Sometimes IKEA gets you 90% of the way there — and that's where we come in. We've mastered the art of hacking IKEA cabinets to overcome their limitations and adapt to your space. Need a cabinet trimmed to fit an awkward corner? Want to integrate a dishwasher panel or build an appliance garage? We've done it all — and if it's something new, we're excited to figure it out. The result is a polished, custom look that nobody would guess started as a flat-pack.

Custom Doors, Elevated Finish

Want the IKEA framework with a truly custom look? Our workshop builds custom cabinet doors and drawer fronts that mount directly onto IKEA cabinet boxes — giving you the quality of custom cabinetry at a fraction of the price. Choose your wood species, your finish, your style. From sleek modern slabs to classic shaker profiles, we build doors that transform IKEA cabinets into something uniquely yours.

Beyond Kitchen & Bath

Frequently Asked Questions

Why hire a contractor for IKEA kitchen installation instead of using IKEA Home Services?

IKEA Home Services covers cabinet assembly and mounting — that’s it. No demo, no plumbing, no electrical, no tile, no flooring, no appliance hookups. You’d need to hire and schedule all of those trades yourself, which is where most people get overwhelmed. When you work with us, you get one point of contact for the entire project — from tearing out the old kitchen to handing you a finished one. We also do IKEA-specific modifications and hacks that the standard installers won’t touch, because they’re only trained on the standard install.

Do you only install IKEA kitchens, or do you handle the full project?

The full project. Demo, structural work if needed, plumbing, electrical, cabinet assembly and installation, countertop coordination, tile backsplash, flooring, appliance hookups, and every finishing detail down to the last handle. Installing cabinets is one step in a kitchen remodel — we manage all of them so you’re not juggling five different contractors and trying to figure out who goes when.

Can you modify IKEA cabinets to fit non-standard spaces?

This is one of the things we’re known for. We regularly trim cabinets to fit awkward corners, adapt them to non-standard ceiling heights, and work around out-of-plumb walls — which is most walls in older Seattle homes. We also build custom features IKEA doesn’t sell, like toe-kick drawers, appliance garages, and integrated storage. And if you want the IKEA framework with a truly custom look, our workshop builds custom cabinet doors and drawer fronts in any wood species or finish that mount right onto the IKEA boxes.

Do you do IKEA bathroom vanity and IKEA PAX installation too?

Absolutely. IKEA’s GODMORGON and ÄNGSJÖN vanity lines are solid quality for the price — we install them along with all the plumbing, lighting, mirrors, and tile work that goes with a bathroom project. We’re also experienced with the PAX wardrobe system, which we’ve installed as bedroom closets, mudroom storage, and walk-in wardrobe buildouts. We’ve also used IKEA’s kitchen cabinet system to build custom bathroom storage, linen closets, and laundry room cabinetry. Basically, if it uses the IKEA system, we’ve probably built it.
