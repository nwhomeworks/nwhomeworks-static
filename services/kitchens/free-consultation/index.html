How Long Does a Kitchen Remodel Take?

Most full kitchen remodels take 6 to 12 weeks from demo day to final walkthrough. A straightforward cabinet and countertop replacement is on the shorter end. Anything with structural changes, plumbing relocation, or custom millwork lands in the longer range.

Here's what to expect for the different scopes we typically handle:

  • Pull-and-replace (cosmetic refresh): 4–6 weeks
  • Mid-range remodel (new layout + appliances): 6–9 weeks
  • Full design-build (wall removals, custom work): 10–14 weeks

We give you a clear week-by-week schedule before we start, and we keep you in the loop the entire way through. If something shifts, you'll hear it from us first.

Do I Have to Move Out During the Remodel?

Almost never. The vast majority of our clients stay in their home through the entire kitchen remodel. We make it work.

Before we start demo, we help you set up a temporary kitchen — usually in a dining room or basement — with the basics: microwave, coffee maker, fridge access, and a workable countertop. We seal off the work zone with floor-to-ceiling dust barriers and zip-doors so the rest of the house stays livable. Shoe covers on every entry. Daily cleanup before our crew leaves.

It's not glamorous for those weeks, but it's manageable — and you'll save the cost of an Airbnb. If you'd rather move out for the rough phase, we can also work with that schedule.

Common Questions

Do I need permits for a kitchen remodel?

If your remodel involves structural changes or major electrical and plumbing work, yes. Cosmetic updates like painting or replacing countertops typically don't. We handle all the permitting for you, so you don't have to navigate the process yourself.

What's included in the free consultation?

We visit your home, discuss your vision and goals, assess the existing space, and talk through options and budget ranges. There's no pressure and no obligation. It's simply a conversation to see if we're the right fit for your project.

What if I already have a designer or some plans?

Great — we'll work with what you have. Bring your designer, your Pinterest board, your napkin sketch, or just a rough idea. We can build from any starting point. If you don't have a designer, we have in-house design that's included in the project.

How does payment work?

We use a milestone-based draw schedule — you pay as agreed phases of the project complete, not all upfront. The exact schedule is outlined in your fixed-price proposal before any work begins.
